# Work And Experience Scene - Wanderer Style Prompts

## Style Analysis From `wanderer_above_the_sea_of_fog.glb`

- The GLB is built from a small number of flat, unlit textured planes and alpha-masked brush meshes, not physically realistic 3D materials.
- Materials are named `brush_Ink`, `brush_ThickPaint`, `brush_Marker`, and a reference image material for Caspar David Friedrich's `Wanderer above the Sea of Fog`.
- The visual language is Romantic oil painting: back-facing solitary figure, dark near-black foreground rock silhouette, pale blue-gray fog sea, distant softened mountain ridges, muted sky, and subtle warm ochre highlights.
- Edges are painterly and imperfect, with visible canvas/craquelure scratches, dry-brush texture, and fog-softened silhouettes.
- Palette: cold blue-gray mist, desaturated slate, charcoal/black foreground, small touches of muted ochre/gold.
- Composition: wide horizontal view, low dark foreground, central heroic human silhouette, massive negative space of fog/sky, distant ridges fading into atmospheric perspective.

## Master Prompt

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: reference image for a 3D model / portfolio work-and-experience portal scene
Primary request: Create a work-and-experience mountain ascent scene in the exact Romantic oil-painting feel of Caspar David Friedrich's "Wanderer above the Sea of Fog", matching the painterly, foggy, solemn style of the provided `wanderer_above_the_sea_of_fog.glb` reference.
Scene/backdrop: A vast mountain slope rising through a sea of pale fog, with layered distant ridges dissolving into blue-gray mist. The scene should feel like a career journey or work timeline without becoming a literal infographic.
Subject: A lone human figure seen from behind, climbing a steep rocky mountain path toward a high summit. The figure is small but iconic, wearing a dark long coat or simple dark explorer silhouette, one hand bracing on rock or holding a walking staff. Along the path are a few subtle milestone stones or small golden markers embedded in the rocks, suggesting work experience progression.
Style/medium: 19th-century Romantic oil painting, Caspar David Friedrich-inspired, painterly brushwork, visible canvas/craquelure texture, dry-brush fog, softly blended atmospheric perspective. The image should also be easy to reinterpret as a stylized GLB: strong silhouettes, layered planes, unlit painterly textures, alpha-mask brush shapes.
Composition/framing: Wide cinematic horizontal composition. Dark rocky foreground at the bottom, mountain path diagonally ascending from lower left or lower center toward the upper right summit. The climber is placed on the lower-middle third, back-facing, looking upward into fog. Large open foggy sky and distant ridges fill the upper half. Keep readable negative space.
Lighting/mood: Misty dawn or overcast alpine light, quiet, ambitious, contemplative, sublime. Low contrast in the background, high contrast in the foreground silhouette. Small muted golden highlights on milestone markers and summit edge.
Color palette: Pale blue-gray, cold white mist, desaturated slate, charcoal black, muted moss-brown rock, tiny ochre/gold accents. Avoid saturated modern colors.
Materials/textures: Oil paint on aged canvas, visible scratched varnish, fine cracks, layered translucent fog, rough dark rocks, dry-brush ridge silhouettes.
Text: none.
Constraints: Must feel like the original Wanderer reference, but the figure is actively ascending instead of standing still. Make the path and milestones subtle and symbolic, not UI icons. Keep the image model-conversion friendly with distinct foreground, midground, background, and fog layers.
Avoid: modern hiking gear, helmets, backpacks, neon colors, clean digital fantasy art, photorealism, anime, cartoon, sharp CGI, text labels, logos, UI panels, literal corporate office objects, oversized glowing icons, sci-fi elements.
